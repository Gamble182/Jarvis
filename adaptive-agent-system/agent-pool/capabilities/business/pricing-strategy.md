# Pricing Strategy Capability

## Domain Knowledge
- Pricing models (subscription, usage-based, tiered, freemium)
- Value-based pricing
- Competitive pricing analysis
- Price sensitivity and elasticity
- SaaS pricing best practices
- Customer lifetime value (LTV)
- Customer acquisition cost (CAC)
- Pricing psychology
- Discount and promotional strategies
- Geographic pricing considerations
- B2B vs B2C pricing
- Price testing and optimization

## Thinking Patterns
- Value alignment: Price reflects value delivered
- Customer segmentation: Different prices for different segments
- Competitive positioning: Price communicates market position
- Simplicity: Easy to understand pricing
- Growth orientation: Pricing supports scaling
- Flexibility: Ability to adjust over time
- Profitability: Covers costs with healthy margins

## Output Formats
- Pricing model recommendation
- Pricing tier structure
- Price points with justification
- Competitive pricing analysis
- Revenue projections by tier
- Pricing page mockup/copy
- Discount/promotion strategy
- Price testing plan

## Required Context
- Value proposition and differentiation
- Target customer segments
- Cost structure (fixed and variable costs)
- Competitive landscape and pricing
- Business goals (growth vs. profitability)
- Customer willingness to pay
- Market positioning (premium, mid-market, budget)

## Collaboration
- **Provides to**:
  - business-strategist (revenue model)
  - marketing-strategist (messaging, positioning)
  - product-manager (feature tier decisions)
  - sales-team (pricing guidance)
- **Receives from**:
  - financial-planner (cost structure, margin requirements)
  - market-researcher (competitive pricing, customer research)
  - business-modeler (value proposition, target segments)

## Key Questions This Capability Answers
- What pricing model best fits this business?
- What should be the price points?
- How many pricing tiers should there be?
- What features belong in each tier?
- How does pricing compare to competitors?
- Is the pricing sustainable and profitable?
- How to handle discounts and promotions?
- Should there be a free tier?

## Specific Considerations for Heizungsbauer SaaS
- Bootstrap-friendly: Low initial price point
- Per-business subscription (not per-user for solo operations)
- Simple tier structure (Solo, Professional, Enterprise)
- Value metric: Number of customers or maintenance visits
- German market pricing norms
- Comparison to manual process costs (Excel, phone calls)
- Price includes what: Email sends, storage, support
