# Business Modeling Capability

## Domain Knowledge
- Business model canvas
- Value proposition design
- Customer segments analysis
- Revenue stream modeling
- Cost structure analysis
- Market sizing (TAM/SAM/SOM)
- Unit economics
- Competitive positioning
- Scalability evaluation

## Thinking Patterns
- Customer-centric problem analysis: Start with customer pain points
- Value chain decomposition: Break down how value is created and delivered
- Competitive positioning: Understand market landscape and differentiation
- Risk assessment: Identify business model risks and mitigation strategies
- Scalability evaluation: Assess growth potential and scaling challenges

## Output Formats
- Business model canvas (JSON/Markdown)
- Value proposition document
- Customer segment profiles
- Revenue model spreadsheet
- Financial projections (basic)
- Market opportunity assessment
- Go/No-Go recommendation with rationale

## Required Context
- Target customer description
- Problem statement being solved
- Competitive landscape information
- Resource constraints (budget, timeline, team)
- Success metrics and goals
- Market characteristics

## Collaboration
- **Provides to**:
  - technical-architect (business requirements, constraints)
  - marketing-strategist (positioning, target segments)
  - financial-planner (revenue assumptions)
  - ux-designer (user personas, value proposition)
- **Receives from**:
  - market-researcher (market data, trends)
  - financial-planner (cost constraints, pricing boundaries)
  - legal-advisor (compliance requirements affecting business model)

## Key Questions This Capability Answers
- Who are the target customers and what problem are we solving?
- What is the value proposition?
- How will revenue be generated?
- What are the key cost drivers?
- Is this business model viable and scalable?
- What are the critical success factors?
